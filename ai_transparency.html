AI Transparency Statement

Last Updated: January 22, 2026

At Digital ABCs, we believe AI should be an engine for empowerment, not a "black box" of mystery. In line with the Australian Governmentâ€™s Voluntary AI Safety Standard, we are committed to being fully transparent about when, how, and why we use Artificial Intelligence.

1. Our Core Principles

Human Oversight

Humans in the Driver's Seat

AI generates drafts; humans make decisions. Every line of code we ship and every strategy we deliver is reviewed, tested, and validated by a human expert. We do not let AI auto-publish critical content without oversight.

Data Privacy

Your Data is Yours

We do not use your private business data to train public AI models. When we use AI tools, we utilize enterprise-grade APIs (like Google Gemini via Vertex AI) that contractually ensure your data remains isolated and ephemeral.

Accessibility

Tech for Everyone

We use AI to make complex tech accessible. From "Linguistic Decoders" that simplify jargon to coding assistants that help us build accessible apps faster, our goal is to lower barriers, not build walls.

2. AI Model Register

Transparency means knowing exactly what is under the hood. Below is a list of the primary AI models and systems currently integrated into our workflows and products.

System/Provider Purpose Data Handling
Google Gemini (via API) Powering the "Linguistic Decoder", drafting code, and summarizing technical documentation. Zero Retention. Data sent via API is not stored by Google for model training.
GitHub Copilot Assisting in writing software code (autocompletion and debugging). Code snippets are processed to provide suggestions but proprietary client logic is kept within our secure repositories.
Vapi AI / Twilio Voice automation for scheduling and initial client intake calls. Transcripts are stored securely in our CRM for human review; voice data is processed for the duration of the call.

3. Limitations and Risks

While AI is powerful, it is not infallible. We want our clients and users to be aware of the following:

  • Hallucinations: Large Language Models (LLMs) can sometimes confidently state incorrect facts. We mitigate this by manually verifying outputs against official documentation (e.g., WCAG standards, legislation).
  • Context: AI lacks real-world lived experience. It acts as a processor of information, not a holder of wisdom. We add the human contextâ€”the "Trish factor"â€”to ensure solutions work for real people.

4. Challenging AI Decisions

We believe in the right to contestability. If you believe an AI-assisted outcome (such as a code recommendation or an automated summary) is incorrect, biased, or harmful, please contact us immediately.

We will perform a manual human review of the output and correct any errors. You can reach us at info@digitalabcs.com.au.
