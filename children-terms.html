Children's Terms Notice

This notice supplements the Terms of Service where a Digital ABCs product is used by, with, or for children in home, school, or educator-led settings.

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

1. Who Should Read This

This notice is primarily for parents, guardians, schools, tutors, and educators. Child users should not be expected to manage legal terms on their own.

2. Supervision and Permission

  • Children should use child-directed or education-focused features with the approval or supervision of a parent, guardian, or authorised educator where appropriate.
  • The adult or institution arranging the child's access is responsible for deciding whether the service is suitable for that child or student.
  • Where the law requires parental consent or supervision, access should not proceed without it.

3. Acceptable Use and Safety

  • Digital ABCs services must not be used to harass, bully, exploit, or intimidate children or other users.
  • Users must not submit unlawful, abusive, sexually explicit, or exploitative content.
  • Adults supervising child use must help ensure the service is used safely and appropriately.

4. AI Outputs and Educational Use

If an AI feature is available in a child or education context:

  • AI outputs should be treated as assistance, not as a substitute for adult judgment.
  • AI-generated content may be incomplete, incorrect, or unsuitable for a particular child.
  • Parents, guardians, and educators should supervise interpretation of AI-assisted outputs.
  • Digital ABCs does not guarantee that AI-generated content will be appropriate for every child or situation.

5. Account, Access, and Device Responsibility

  • The responsible adult or institution should keep access credentials and devices secure.
  • Where a child is using a shared or educator-controlled account, the adult or institution remains responsible for how that access is managed.
  • We may suspend or restrict access where misuse, safety risk, or legal concerns arise.

6. Reporting Concerns

If a parent, guardian, or educator believes a child-facing feature is unsafe, misleading, or inappropriate, contact us promptly so we can review it.
