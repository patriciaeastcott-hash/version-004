Brand Overview

Two businesses, one owner, one mission: find what's broken and fix it. These guidelines keep both brands consistent while letting each one have its own identity.

Digital ABCs

App Development & Tech Services

We deep-dive into business workflows, find the bottlenecks, and build custom applications to eliminate them. We also offer general tech support and consulting.

Personality: Direct, practical, no-nonsense. Technical expertise without the jargon. Honest about what will and won't work.

BBB Logistics

Beach to Bridge to Bush Logistics

Flexible delivery services for local businesses. Single and multi-drop deliveries, seasonal support, returns handling. Transparent pricing, reliable service.

Personality: Warm, reliable, straightforward. A real person who loves driving, knows the roads, and gets your stuff where it needs to go.

Brand Relationship

Digital ABCs is the parent brand. BBB Logistics operates as a separate service under the same business owner. They share a website but maintain distinct visual identities:

  • Digital ABCs uses a cool palette (navy, purple, green) conveying professionalism and technical precision
  • BBB Logistics uses a warm palette (orange, amber, cream) conveying approachability and energy
  • Both brands share the same typography (Inter), layout system, and component patterns
  • Both maintain the same honest, direct tone of voice

Brand Values

  • Honesty first - Tell people what will work and what won't. No upselling, no spin.
  • Show, don't sell - Build useful free tools. Let the work speak for itself.
  • Keep it simple - If it needs a training course, it's failed. This applies to design too.
  • Respect people's time - Clear pricing, transparent processes, no hidden fees.
  • Real over polished - Authentic over corporate. Relatable over impressive.

Voice & Tone

How both brands speak. The voice is consistent - honest, direct, practical. The warmth adjusts per brand.

Brand Voice Principles

  • Talk like a real person. Not a brochure, not a LinkedIn post, not a press release. Write how you'd explain it to someone over coffee.
  • Be direct. Say what you mean. Don't hide behind jargon or corporate speak. "We build apps" not "We deliver bespoke digital transformation solutions."
  • Be honest. If something won't work, say so. If there's a downside, mention it. People trust honesty over polish.
  • Show empathy. We understand that running a business is hard, that systems break, that time is money. Speak to the frustration, then offer the solution.
  • No hard sell. State what we do, what it costs, and let people decide. Pressure tactics don't belong here.

Tone by Brand

Digital ABCs Tone
  • Professional but not corporate
  • Technical confidence without arrogance
  • Problem-focused: "Here's what's broken, here's how to fix it"
  • Slight edge: "If it needs a training course, we've failed"
  • Educational: Explain things, don't gatekeep knowledge
BBB Logistics Tone
  • Warmer and more personal
  • Reliable and straightforward
  • Practical: "Here's what it costs, here's what you get"
  • Local feel: Built from the ground up, community-minded
  • No-drama: Get the job done, keep it simple

Words We Use / Words We Don't

Words We Use

  • Build, fix, solve, find
  • Bottleneck, friction, waste
  • Simple, clear, honest
  • Your business, your workflow
  • Straight answer, no BS
  • Free tool, transparent pricing

Words We Avoid

  • Leverage, synergy, optimise
  • Bespoke, holistic, paradigm
  • Disruptive, cutting-edge, innovative
  • Stakeholders, verticals, scalable
  • Touch base, circle back, deep dive (ironic, yes)
  • AI-powered (unless explaining actual AI use)

Do's & Don'ts

Quick reference for keeping both brands consistent.

Visual Design

Do

  • Use consistent border-radius (8px standard, 12-16px for cards)
  • Maintain generous whitespace between sections (4rem+)
  • Use the defined colour palette - no off-brand colours
  • Keep BBB Logistics warm (orange/cream) and Digital ABCs cool (navy/purple)
  • Use emoji icons for app cards (they're part of the brand's approachable feel)
  • Ensure all interactive elements have hover transitions
  • Use the dot-grid pattern for Digital ABCs hero sections

Don't

  • Mix BBB orange with Digital ABCs navy in the same component
  • Use sharp corners (0 border-radius)
  • Add heavy drop shadows - keep shadows subtle
  • Use more than 2 accent colours in one section
  • Place text directly on busy image backgrounds without overlay
  • Use icon libraries - stick with emoji for consistency
  • Add animations longer than 0.3s - keep transitions snappy

Content & Copy

Do

  • Always show pricing upfront where possible
  • Describe what the problem is before the solution
  • Use Australian English (colour, organisation, analyse)
  • Include real purchase/contact links on every app and service
  • Keep CTAs action-oriented ("Get a Quote", "Use Now", "Learn More")
  • Disclose AI usage transparently

Don't

  • Hide pricing behind "contact us" unless genuinely project-dependent
  • Use stock photography - keep it real or use no images
  • Overuse exclamation marks or ALL CAPS outside badges
  • Promise results that can't be guaranteed
  • Use "we" when "I" is more authentic (About page is personal)
  • Add testimonials unless they're genuine and attributable

Accessibility

Do

  • Include skip-to-content link on every page
  • Ensure all interactive elements have visible focus states (purple outline)
  • Use semantic HTML (header, nav, main, footer, section)
  • Provide alt text for all images
  • Maintain colour contrast ratios (WCAG AAA: 7:1 normal text, 4.5:1 large text)
  • Use aria-labels where link text isn't descriptive

Don't

  • Use colour alone to convey meaning (always pair with text/icons)
  • Remove focus outlines without providing alternatives
  • Use generic link text ("click here", "read more" without context)
  • Auto-play any media
  • Nest interactive elements inside other interactive elements
  • Use text smaller than 0.75rem for any functional content
