Digital ABCs is a founder-led app studio built around family, time, and real constraints.

The business is based in Western Sydney and focused on building self-quantification apps for adults and educational apps for children. It exists because one founder needed to build a way forward that could support her children without disappearing from their lives to do it.

Founder's mission

The studio story starts with an autodidactic neurodivergent single mum in Western Sydney.

Digital ABCs is founder-led by Trish Eastcott, who learned to build software by doing it. No formal computer science path, no padded founder mythology, and no pretending the work happened under easy conditions. The studio grew out of lived pressure, pattern recognition, and a refusal to accept that broken systems should stay broken.

That background shapes the product direction. Adult-facing work focuses on self-understanding, clearer patterns, and practical decision support. Children's work focuses on educational tools that help young learners build confidence through reading, cooperation, and structured support.

"Show my children you don't need to sacrifice the relationships with people you love to provide for them."

The goal is personal, practical, and not abstract.

Digital ABCs is part of a real Western Sydney journey: buying a house without missing her children growing up. That goal forces the business to stay anchored in work that is maintainable, honest, and worth building over time. Every product decision sits underneath that larger test. If a project does not support a real life, it is not the right project.

What the studio is building

A small focused portfolio for adults and children.

The studio is not positioning itself as a general service provider anymore. It is narrowing around products that help adults understand themselves better and help children learn in ways that respect different starting points.

For adults

Linguistic Decoder and the Compliance Tool represent the adult-facing side of the studio: conversation analysis, pattern recognition, and clearer orientation around information, data, and risk.

For children

Bright Spark and the Minecraft add-ons stream are being shaped to support reading, teamwork, cooperation, and adaptable learning pathways without flattening every child into the same pace or style.

For families who need practicality

The studio is built from a lived understanding that time, money, attention, and access are limited. That is why plain language, transparent legal framing, and honest status updates are part of the public product surface.

Working principles

The business promise is straightforward.

Clarity over performance

Pages should say what exists, what is in beta, what is still being built, and what the trust surfaces actually cover.

Useful products over generic services

The site is being repositioned around a focused app studio so the public story matches the work that matters most now.

Family-aware ambition

Growth only counts if it does not cost the relationships the founder is trying to protect while building the business.
