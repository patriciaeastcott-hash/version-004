The Person Behind the Apps

I build apps that fill the gaps in everyday life. If they make my life easier, I figure they might make yours easier too.

The Real Version

I'm Patricia. I'm a single mum. First Nations. I've got bipolar, CPTSD, and ADHD. If you've ever dealt with Centrelink while trying to juggle mental health and parenting, you already know what that's like. It's exhausting, and the system isn't built for people like us.

I struggle with authority and being managed. Always have. Something about being told what to do when I can see a better way just doesn't sit right. That's probably why I ended up building my own things instead of fitting into someone else's box.

Here's the thing about being neurodivergent: my brain sees gaps everywhere. Where most people see "this is just how it's done," I see the thing that should be easier but isn't. The calculator that doesn't exist. The tool that almost works but not quite. So I build them.

It started just for me - little tools to make my own life less chaotic. Then I figured if they help me, maybe they'll help someone else too. So I started putting them out there. Everything's free. No sign-ups, no upselling, no catch. Just tools that do what they say.

Beach to Bridge to Bush Logistics

The other thing I do happened by accident. I started driving for an international cuisine franchise because I needed flexible income that didn't require me to sit in an office pretending to be normal for 8 hours. Turns out, I love being on the road. The driving, the travel, the freedom of it.

Along the way, I started noticing local businesses that needed delivery support - especially seasonal stuff. A restaurant that gets slammed during summer. A farm shop that needs extra runs during harvest. Small operators who can't justify a full-time driver but need someone reliable when it counts. So Beach to Bridge to Bush Logistics was born.

Finding opportunity in the gaps is kind of my thing. When everything went sideways during the hard times, I looked at what I had - a car, time between school pickups, and a willingness to hustle - and turned it into something that works.

Why I Do This

For My Kids

I want to show my children that you don't have to sacrifice the relationships with the people you love to provide for them. That there's a way to make a living that doesn't mean missing their school plays or being too burnt out to actually be present when you're home. I'm building proof that it's possible.

For People Like Me

The world wasn't designed for neurodivergent people, or single parents, or anyone who doesn't fit the mould. I build tools for the people who just need things to work - without the corporate price tag or the corporate nonsense. If something I made saves you ten minutes or one headache, that's a win.

Because My Brain Won't Stop

I see inefficiencies everywhere. The calculator that should exist but doesn't. The conversion tool that's buried behind three sign-up walls. My brain won't let me ignore it, so I just build the thing. It's cheaper than therapy.
