Decision Journal

Track your decisions and outcomes to improve your judgment over time. Data proves which decision-making strategies actually work for you.

✍️

Clarify your thinking

Writing down your reasoning before deciding forces clarity. What are the options? What's your confidence level?

⏰

Track outcomes

Set a check-in date. When it arrives, rate the outcome. What would you do differently? Build a feedback loop.

📈

Improve judgment

After enough decisions, see the data: Are you overconfident? Does your gut actually work? What contexts lead to better outcomes?

How it works

1

Log the decision

Record what you're deciding, the options you considered, your reasoning, and your confidence level (0-100%).

2

Set a check-in

When will you know how it turned out? 1 week? 6 months? The app reminds you when it's time to evaluate.

3

Rate the outcome

How did it turn out vs expectations? What did you learn? Would you decide differently? Close the loop.

What you'll be able to do

Decision logging

Structured templates that capture what matters without being burdensome.

  • What's the decision?
  • Options considered
  • Your reasoning
  • Confidence level (0-100%)
  • Categories: Work, Personal, Financial, Health

Outcome tracking

When your check-in date arrives, the app prompts you to evaluate.

  • Rate: Much worse → Much better than expected
  • What actually happened?
  • What would you do differently?
  • Lessons learned

Calibration analysis

Are you well-calibrated? Does your confidence match actual outcomes?

  • Confidence vs outcome charts
  • Overconfidence detection
  • Underconfidence detection
  • "Lower your estimates by ~25%"

Pattern insights

After enough data, see what contexts lead to your best decisions.

  • Decision by category performance
  • Gut vs analysed decisions
  • Time-pressure impact
  • Stress context correlation

The insights you'll get

After logging enough decisions and outcomes, you'll see patterns like these:

"Your gut instinct is right 67% of the time in personal decisions, but only 45% in financial ones."

"Decisions made under stress have 40% worse outcomes. You might want to delay important choices when stressed."

"You're overconfident in work decisions. You predicted 90% success but actually succeeded 65% of the time."

Who it's for

Professionals

Managers, founders, investors — anyone making frequent high-stakes decisions who wants to systematically improve their judgment.

Career builders

Job changes, project choices, relationship investments. Track the big decisions that shape your life and learn from them.

Self-improvers

Anyone working on better judgment. Replace "I think I'm good at decisions" with "here's the data on my decisions."
