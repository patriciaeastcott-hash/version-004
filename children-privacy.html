Children's Privacy Notice

This notice supplements the Privacy Policy where a Digital ABCs service is directed to children, used in home learning, or used in school or educator-led contexts. It does not replace the main Privacy Policy.

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

1. When This Notice Applies

This notice applies where:

  • a Digital ABCs product is designed for children or students
  • a product is used in school, tutoring, or educator-led settings
  • a parent, guardian, or educator is using a Digital ABCs child-focused feature on behalf of a child

2. Parental or Guardian Role

Where required by law, we expect a parent, guardian, or authorised educator to approve or supervise the collection and use of personal information relating to a child.

  • We may seek verifiable parental or guardian consent before collecting personal information from a child where required.
  • Parents and guardians may contact us to review, correct, or request deletion of a child's information.
  • Educators or schools using a Digital ABCs product are responsible for ensuring they have the authority needed to use the service with students.

3. Data Minimisation

We aim to collect the minimum information reasonably necessary for the feature or educational purpose involved.

  • We do not ask children to provide more personal information than is needed for the service to work.
  • Where a feature can work with de-identified or low-sensitivity information, that is preferred.
  • We expect users, parents, guardians, and educators to avoid submitting unnecessary sensitive information into child-facing tools.

4. AI Features and Children

If an AI-assisted feature is available in a child-facing or education-facing context, we treat that as a higher-care scenario.

  • We aim to limit processing to the minimum information required for the feature.
  • We do not use children's submitted content to train our own AI systems.
  • Where third-party AI services are involved, we aim to use configurations that restrict training use and retain as little data as reasonably possible.
  • Parents, guardians, and educators should supervise children where AI-generated responses are involved.

5. Advertising and Profiling

Digital ABCs does not currently run child-focused services on the basis of behavioural advertising. We do not intend child-facing experiences to depend on tracking children across services for advertising profiles.

6. School and Educator Use

  • Schools, tutors, and educators are responsible for their own consent, authority, and recordkeeping obligations where they use our services with children.
  • Where Digital ABCs acts as a service provider in an educational setting, we aim to process student information only for the agreed educational or product function.
  • We do not intend student information to be used for unrelated advertising or profiling.

7. Parent and Guardian Rights

Parents and guardians may contact us to:

  • request access to personal information we hold about their child
  • request correction of inaccurate information
  • request deletion, subject to legal or operational retention limits
  • withdraw consent where consent is the basis for processing

Related pages: Privacy Policy, Children's Terms Notice, AI Transparency

8. Contact

Questions about children's privacy can be sent to info@digitalabcs.com.au.
