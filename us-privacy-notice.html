U.S. Privacy Notice

This notice supplements the Privacy Policy for users in the United States. It is intended to improve clarity about categories of information, disclosures, child privacy context, and request pathways.

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

1. Scope

This notice applies to U.S.-based visitors, customers, beta testers, and app users where U.S. privacy expectations or laws are relevant to our handling of personal information.

2. Categories of Information We May Collect

  • Identifiers and contact details such as name, email address, phone number, and business name
  • Commercial or service information such as project details, billing information, and service history
  • Internet or device information such as browser, operating system, device details, and usage information
  • Content submitted through apps, support requests, or AI-assisted features
  • Approximate location information inferred from IP address or service context

3. Why We Use Information

  • to provide services, apps, support, and product access
  • to process payments and maintain records
  • to improve product quality, stability, and usability
  • to communicate with users about enquiries, updates, support, or beta access
  • to comply with legal, tax, security, and fraud-prevention obligations

4. Disclosure to Service Providers and Others

We may disclose personal information to service providers or contractors that help us operate the business, including payment processors, hosting providers, analytics providers, communication providers, and AI service providers where a feature requires it.

We may also disclose information where required by law or where reasonably necessary to protect rights, safety, or the integrity of the service.

5. Sale or Sharing for Advertising

We do not sell personal information. Based on our current operations, we do not share personal information for cross-context behavioural advertising in the way that many U.S. state privacy laws describe.

6. Retention

We retain information only for as long as reasonably necessary for the purpose it was collected, and where required for legal, tax, security, warranty, or dispute-management reasons. See the main Privacy Policy for the general retention approach.

7. Children's Privacy in the U.S.

If a Digital ABCs service is directed to children or used in a child-focused setting, we refer users to the Children's Privacy Notice and child-related sections of the main Privacy Policy.

Where applicable, we expect parental or guardian involvement and aim to seek parental consent before collecting personal information from a child where the law requires it.

U.S. privacy requests: If an applicable U.S. state privacy law gives you rights such as access, correction, deletion, or appeal, contact us and we will assess the request against the laws that apply to our handling of your information.

8. Contact and Requests

Privacy requests can be sent to info@digitalabcs.com.au. We may ask for information needed to verify your identity before acting on a request.
