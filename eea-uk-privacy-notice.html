EEA / UK Privacy Notice

This notice supplements the Privacy Policy where EEA or UK data protection expectations apply. It is intended to clarify lawful bases, rights, and international transfer framing.

Last Updated: April 23, 2026

1. Scope

This notice is relevant where Digital ABCs or BBB Logistics offers services to individuals in the EEA or UK, or where EEA/UK personal data is otherwise processed in a context where those rules may apply.

2. Controller and Location

Digital ABCs and BBB Logistics are operated from New South Wales, Australia. Unless otherwise stated, the business is managed from Australia and uses service providers in Australia and other jurisdictions, including the United States.

3. Lawful Bases We May Rely On

Depending on the context, we may rely on one or more of the following lawful bases:

  • Contract: where processing is needed to provide a requested service, product, or support interaction
  • Consent: where you choose to opt into a feature, beta, communication, or other optional processing
  • Legitimate interests: where processing is reasonably necessary to operate, secure, improve, or support the service and those interests are not overridden by your rights
  • Legal obligation: where we must keep records, respond to legal requirements, or meet compliance obligations

4. Your Rights

Where applicable, you may have rights including:

  • access to your personal data
  • correction of inaccurate data
  • erasure in certain circumstances
  • restriction of processing in certain circumstances
  • data portability where the law provides it
  • objection to certain processing, including some legitimate-interest based processing
  • withdrawal of consent where consent is the basis relied on

5. International Transfers

Personal data may be processed in Australia and through service providers in other countries, including the United States. Where EEA/UK transfer rules apply, we aim to use contractual, organisational, and technical safeguards appropriate to the processing context.

Important: Digital ABCs does not currently state that it has appointed a separate EEA representative, UK representative, or Data Protection Officer for these services unless such an appointment is expressly published.

6. Complaints and Contact

You can contact us at info@digitalabcs.com.au to exercise rights or ask privacy questions. If you believe your rights have been infringed and EEA/UK law applies, you may also have the right to complain to a relevant supervisory authority.
