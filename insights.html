Founder story

Built by someone who saw the patterns and needed to name them.

Digital ABCs is founder-led and built under real-world constraints: family responsibilities, limited time, self-taught technical work, tight money, late nights, hard decisions, and a stubborn refusal to believe that providing for relationships should require sacrificing yourself inside them.

The work started from a simple belief: people make better decisions when they can see what is actually happening. Not the performance. Not the excuse. Not the emotional fog machine someone wheeled in and switched on. Linguistic Decoder applies that belief to conversations. Future products will only launch when the trust, governance, and product readiness are strong enough to support them.

No inflated certainty

Digital ABCs explains what AI can support and where human judgement still matters. Linguistic Decoder is part of the puzzle, not the whole toolbox, the tradie trailer, and the entire job site.

Release carefully

Social Skill Simulation is under OT review. Contract Compass comes next. Anchor waits for greater trust in AI. Useful products are not rushed out just because someone found a launch button.

Plain-language trust

Privacy, redaction, governance, and model use should be understandable before someone submits anything. No legal fog. No corporate interpretive dance. Just the parts people need to know.

Useful over flashy

The goal is clarity that helps people act, not complexity that makes the product feel clever. If it does not help someone make a better decision, it can get back in the ideas folder.
